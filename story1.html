Ashish

On his first day Ashish clutched a small notebook like a shield. Reading felt like chasing shadows—letters seemed to disappear when he tried to sound them out. One mentor sat with him under the tree and quietly guided his finger along each line. "Chalo, dhire dhire," she said. The first time he read a full sentence aloud at home, his mother cried softly with pride.

Now Ashish volunteers to help younger children practice their reading. He often says, "Main padhunga," with a grin that used to be unimaginable. That transformation—fear to pride—shows what patient, consistent mentoring does.
