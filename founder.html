Meet the Founder

Tanisha Satapathy

I started Bright Futures in 2022 as a student who wanted to bring learning to my own neighborhood. Balancing school and this project taught me leadership, resilience, and the power of community. Our goal is to create sustainable, community-owned learning spaces where volunteers, parents, and local partners work together.

Most participants are local students and teachers from under-resourced backgrounds; our model is intentionally low-cost and relies on donations, volunteer time, and small partnerships. We measure success not only in grades but in confidence, school enrollment, and long-term community involvement.
